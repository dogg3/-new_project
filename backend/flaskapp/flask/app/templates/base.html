The data processing

There are different data types available for analysis in football. These are geospatialrelated data elicited with cameras and trackings. Then there are the event data provided often raw and unprocessed. The data tells how many passes or shots a player have in the season and can then be compared to other players absolute values. However these kind of metrics are often not effective. It is hard to look at isolated data points and draw a conclusion about the player. We took it a step further and analyzed the data of the top 5 leauges to see how the distrubtions of the players event-data were. By studying the data we found positions specific metrics which were clustering the player much better.
